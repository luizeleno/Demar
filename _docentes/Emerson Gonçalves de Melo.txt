Reveiced his PhD degree in Electrical Engineering from the Polytechnic School of the University of São Paulo (2017) and holds a postdoctoral degree in Applied Physics from the State University of Campinas (2019). Dedicated to studies concerning optical phenomena and devices based on periodic nanostructures in dielectric and metallic materials. Research activities includes theoretical studies supported by numerical and analytical methods, optimizations of photonic devices, nanofabrication processes and optical/physical characterizations. Interested in photonics, plasmonics and nanotechnology.
